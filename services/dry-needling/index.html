What it is

About Dry Needling.

Dry needling, also known as Intramuscular Stimulation (IMS), uses fine acupuncture-style needles to release trigger points in tight muscles. It is performed by registered physiotherapists trained in the technique and is integrated with manual therapy and exercise.

Who it helps.

  • Patients with persistent muscular tension
  • Trigger point related headaches or neck pain
  • Movement restrictions related to muscle tightness
  • Athletes recovering from overuse injuries

Conditions treated.

Myofascial PainNeck PainUpper Back PainTension HeadachesTennis ElbowGolfer's ElbowPlantar Fasciitis

What to expect at your first visit.

  • Assessment to identify trigger points
  • Insertion of fine needles directly into the muscle
  • Brief twitch responses as the muscle releases
  • Mild post-treatment soreness, often resolved with movement

Why Alliance Physio for Dry Needling.

  • Performed by registered physiotherapists with specific dry needling training
  • Integrated with manual therapy and exercise rehab
  • Used selectively, when clinically indicated

Frequently asked questions.

Both use similar needles but the philosophy differs. Dry needling targets specific muscle trigger points based on a Western anatomical framework. Acupuncture is rooted in traditional Chinese medicine theory.
Most patients feel a brief twitch when the needle reaches the trigger point. Discomfort is typically short-lived.
When provided by a physiotherapist, it is covered under physiotherapy benefits in most plans.
